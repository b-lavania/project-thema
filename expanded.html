Last-mile delivery & moving · 2024–present
Your ops team shouldn't spend
an hour quoting every job
Built for Moovez  ·  Now available as Quotely — a standalone SaaS for logistics operators

The problem

Manual job quoting is a hidden tax on operations. Every estimate that takes an hour — built from phone calls, visual guesswork, and spreadsheet arithmetic — is an hour not spent on the next job.

Worse, manual quotes are unreliable. When actual job times diverge from quoted times, you get billing disputes, unhappy customers, and a support queue that grows with every booking.

What's possible instead

The same quoting problem is solvable with historical job data, computer vision, and operations research — without replacing your existing workflow wholesale.

Three interconnected systems were designed, prototyped, and shipped to production for Moovez. The result is now sold as Quotely to other operators in the space.

How it works

Computer Vision
Customers photograph items — the system quotes the job

A CV-based recommendation tool estimates volume, weight class, and handling complexity from photos. No dispatcher call required to start a quote.

Operations Research
Algorithm sets the time, crew size, and truck load

A predictive framework solves job duration, optimal mover count, and 3D bin packing for truck loading simultaneously — trained on your historical job data.

Conversational AI
SMS agent handles support so your team doesn't have to

A conversational SMS agent handles booking confirmations, reschedules, and common queries — integrated with your ops backend for real-time job status.

This system went from concept to production in a single engagement. It's now a commercialized SaaS product — which means the architecture is proven, not theoretical. If your operations have similar shape, the same approach applies.
