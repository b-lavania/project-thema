The problem

Moovez, a last-mile delivery and moving brokerage, was quoting jobs manually. Each estimate took a dispatcher an average of one hour, a combination of phone calls, visual guesswork, and spreadsheet arithmetic.

The resulting quotes were unreliable. Actual job times varied widely from quoted times, creating billing disputes, unpredictable crew scheduling, and a customer support burden that scaled linearly with volume.

The approach

Rather than layering tooling onto a broken process, the entire estimation and operations workflow was rebuilt from first principles, using historical job data, computer vision, and operations research.

Three interconnected systems were defined, prototyped, and shipped to production. Each addressed a distinct failure point in the original process.

Systems built

Computer vision
Visual cost estimation tool

Replaced manual inventory calls with a CV-based recommendation tool. Customers photograph items; the model estimates volume, weight class, and handling complexity to generate an initial quote.

Operations research
Job-time & mover allocation algorithm

Engineered a predictive framework combining three NP-hard sub-problems: job duration estimation, optimal mover count, and 3D bin packing for truck loading. Trained on historical job performance data.

Conversational AI
SMS-based support & ops agent

Built and deployed a conversational SMS agent handling booking confirmations, schedule changes, and common support queries. Integrated with operations backend for real-time job status.

The OR algorithm was engineered end-to-end by a single contributor, from mining historical job data and defining the predictive framework, through rapid prototyping and validation, to driving engineering collaboration for production deployment. The resulting system is now commercialized as Quotely, sold as a standalone SaaS product to other operators.
