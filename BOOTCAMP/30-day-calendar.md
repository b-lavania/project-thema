# 30-Day Calendar — PM Shock Cycle Bootcamp

## Legend

- **D1-7**: Decision (hard product decision)
- **WA**: Written artifact
- **VR**: Verbal communication rep
- **BC**: Backlog cut
- **AR**: Anti-defensiveness rewrite
- **SB**: Scoreboard published
- **PA**: Public artifact (weekly mandatory)
- **HIIT**: High-intensity drill

---

## Week 1: Brutal Clarity
Theme: Strip away fuzziness. Make calls. Speak plainly.

### Day 1 (Monday)
- **Focus**: Establish the protocol. First diagnosis.
- **Block A**: Write your first 10-sentence diagnosis on a product you know well
- **Block B**: Record a 3-minute spoken diagnosis. No notes.
- **Block C**: Rewrite your LinkedIn headline and About section to the single identity
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 1 — 15-minute teardown of a logistics app
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 3 — Anti-defensiveness rewrite on your current positioning
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 2 (Tuesday)
- **Focus**: Diagnosis speed. Cut throat-clearing.
- **Block A**: Diagnose a product you've never used in 10 sentences
- **Block B**: Write a one-page recommendation memo with zero hedging
- **Block C**: Cut 5 items from your current task backlog
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 2 — Recommendation under constraint (pricing decision)
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 6 — Executive compression of yesterday's memo
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 3 (Wednesday)
- **Focus**: Two-way vs one-way doors. Decision classification.
- **Block A**: Classify 5 past product decisions as reversible or irreversible
- **Block B**: Write an executive update on a current work stream
- **Block C**: Build a visible backlog with metric tags for all active work
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 1 — Teardown of a competitor in your domain
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 4 — Operator translation (same problem, 3 languages)
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 4 (Thursday)
- **Focus**: State once. No self-protection language.
- **Block A**: Write a controversial recommendation. No hedging.
- **Block B**: Deliver a verbal roadmap defense (recorded)
- **Block C**: Rewrite one case study from your portfolio in simpler language
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 3 — Anti-defensiveness rewrite of an old resume bullet
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 5 — Backlog triage: 10 items → 2
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 5 (Friday)
- **Focus**: Pattern recognition. Where do you hide behind nuance?
- **Block A**: Diagnose 3 products for the same underlying problem class
- **Block B**: Write a stakeholder memo stating a decision before it's contentious
- **Block C**: Cut all positioning language that needs defending
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 7 — Hostile stakeholder response (CEO skeptical of your bet)
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 2 — 10-minute recommendation on a feature kill/keep
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 6 (Saturday)
- **Focus**: Consolidation. What's the pattern?
- **Block A**: Write a one-page summary: "What I learned about my own judgment this week"
- **Block B**: Record a 5-minute product brief on your domain without slides
- **Block C**: Rebuild your backlog: everything must have a metric
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 1 — Speed teardown (10 minutes, not 15)
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 6 — Compression under pressure
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 7 (Sunday)
- **Focus**: Weekly audit and public artifact
- **Morning**: Complete weekly audit
- **Afternoon**: **PUBLIC ARTIFACT #1** — Publish a product teardown
- **Evening**: Plan Week 2. Set the one thing to fix.
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB, PA

---

## Week 2: Prioritization and Product Brutality
Theme: Kill the feature factory. Everything must fight for its life.

### Day 8 (Monday)
- **Focus**: Force ranking. There is no tie for first.
- **Block A**: Force-rank your entire backlog. No ties allowed.
- **Block B**: Write "Why we are not doing this" memo for the #3-5 items
- **Block C**: Build a prioritization board with impact/effort axes
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 5 — Aggressive triage: 15 items → 3
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 2 — Recommendation: which 2 of 5 features to kill
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 9 (Tuesday)
- **Focus**: Metric tie-in. No number, no ticket.
- **Block A**: Tag every backlog item with the metric it moves and expected impact
- **Block B**: Write a metric-driven roadmap defense
- **Block C**: Cut 50% of your active task list
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 1 — Teardown focused on metric quality
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 3 — Rewrite a metric claim to remove projection/softness
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 10 (Wednesday)
- **Focus**: Roadmap defense. Top item must beat next three combined.
- **Block A**: Write a one-page defense of your #1 priority vs #2-4
- **Block B**: Record a verbal roadmap defense (5 minutes)
- **Block C**: Map dependencies for top 3 items. What's blocked?
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 4 — Translate a roadmap into operator and exec language
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 6 — Compress a complex roadmap into 3 bullets
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 11 (Thursday)
- **Focus**: Kill-a-darling. The attractive low-impact item.
- **Block A**: Identify your most intellectually interesting but low-impact task. Kill it.
- **Block B**: Write the kill memo: why it's attractive, why it's wrong
- **Block C**: Reallocate the time to your #1 item
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 2 — Quick decision: kill or keep a beloved feature
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 5 — Emergency triage under time pressure
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 12 (Friday)
- **Focus**: WIP limit enforcement. Max 2 in flight.
- **Block A**: Audit everything in flight. Pause everything beyond #2.
- **Block B**: Write stakeholder memo explaining the WIP pause
- **Block C**: Design a system to prevent WIP creep
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 7 — Respond to angry stakeholder about paused work
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 3 — Remove hedging from the pause memo
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 13 (Saturday)
- **Focus**: Mid-week consolidation. What's actually moving?
- **Block A**: Score every active item by evidence of movement
- **Block B**: Write a "what's working / what's dead" memo
- **Block C**: Update backlog. Cut anything not moving.
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 1 — Teardown your own product/process
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 6 — Compress your week into 3 bullets
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 14 (Sunday)
- **Focus**: Weekly audit and public artifact
- **Morning**: Complete weekly audit
- **Afternoon**: **PUBLIC ARTIFACT #2** — Publish a prioritization framework or kill memo
- **Evening**: Plan Week 3.
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB, PA

## Week 3: Stakeholder Combat and Commercial Instinct
Theme: Stop being a solo thinker. Drive aligned motion.

### Day 15 (Monday)
- **Focus**: Pre-alignment. Explain before it becomes contentious.
- **Block A**: Write a pre-alignment memo for a real or hypothetical decision
- **Block B**: Record a verbal walkthrough of the decision for a stakeholder
- **Block C**: Map all stakeholders for a real or hypothetical project
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 7 — Pre-alignment memo under pressure (10 min)
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 4 — Same decision in 3 languages
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 16 (Tuesday)
- **Focus**: Commercial translation. Revenue, retention, cost, trust.
- **Block A**: Rewrite a product decision purely in commercial language
- **Block B**: Write a stakeholder update in money terms
- **Block C**: Build a simple unit-economics model for a decision
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 2 — Recommendation with revenue impact
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 3 — Remove all product jargon from one paragraph
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 17 (Wednesday)
- **Focus**: Conflict scenarios. Speed vs quality vs stability.
- **Block A**: Write a decision memo balancing CEO speed, eng quality, ops stability
- **Block B**: Record a verbal disagreement-and-commit practice
- **Block C**: Write a "disagree and commit" memo
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 7 — Hostile response to your speed/quality tradeoff
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 5 — Triage when everything is "urgent"
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 18 (Thursday)
- **Focus**: Explicit asks. Every conversation ends with one.
- **Block A**: Write 5 explicit asks for different stakeholder types
- **Block B**: Practice asking for budget, headcount, timeline, or scope change
- **Block C**: Document the responses you expect vs what you'd accept
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 4 — Ask for something in 3 modes (PM, exec, operator)
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 6 — Compress an ask into one bullet
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 19 (Friday)
- **Focus**: Disagree-and-commit. When the call isn't yours.
- **Block A**: Write a memo for a decision you disagree with but will execute
- **Block B**: Record a verbal explanation of why you're committing anyway
- **Block C**: Design a checkpoint to verify if the decision was wrong
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 7 — Respond to "I told you so" after a committed decision fails
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 2 — Quick decision: when to escalate vs when to commit
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 20 (Saturday)
- **Focus**: Stakeholder muscle review. Where are you still weak?
- **Block A**: Write a self-assessment of stakeholder management skills
- **Block B**: Record a mock board update (3 minutes)
- **Block C**: Build a stakeholder communication plan for a project
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 1 — Teardown a stakeholder communication failure
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 6 — Compress stakeholder map into 3 bullets
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 21 (Sunday)
- **Focus**: Weekly audit and public artifact
- **Morning**: Complete weekly audit
- **Afternoon**: **PUBLIC ARTIFACT #3** — Publish a stakeholder pre-alignment template or commercial translation example
- **Evening**: Plan Week 4.
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB, PA

## Week 4: Synthesis, Shipping, and Executive Presence
Theme: Operate like a high-trust lead, not a clever contributor.

### Day 22 (Monday)
- **Focus**: Strategy memo. One page, one bet, one metric.
- **Block A**: Write a one-page strategy memo for your domain
- **Block B**: Record a 5-minute verbal brief without slides
- **Block C**: Get peer review on the memo (or self-critique)
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 6 — Compress a strategy deck into one page
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 7 — Respond to skepticism about your strategy bet
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 23 (Tuesday)
- **Focus**: Portfolio-grade product memo. The deliverable.
- **Block A**: Draft the full product memo (market, problem, solution, metrics)
- **Block B**: Write the exec summary (3 bullets, 1 ask)
- **Block C**: Build the metric model
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 3 — Anti-defensiveness rewrite of risk section
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 4 — Translate the memo into operator language
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 24 (Wednesday)
- **Focus**: Market thesis. Why now, why you, why this domain.
- **Block A**: Write a crisp market thesis for logistics/field service AI
- **Block B**: Record a verbal pitch of the thesis
- **Block C**: Map the competitive landscape in one page
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 1 — Speed teardown of a competitor's positioning
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 5 — Triage: which market signal to trust vs ignore
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 25 (Thursday)
- **Focus**: Product strategy artifact. Metrics, tradeoffs, sequencing.
- **Block A**: Build the strategy artifact with explicit tradeoffs
- **Block B**: Write the sequencing rationale
- **Block C**: Design the rollback plan for each bet
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 2 — Decision: which bet to sequence first
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 6 — Compress strategy into exec summary
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 26 (Friday)
- **Focus**: Verbal delivery. Inevitable, not tentative.
- **Block A**: Rehearse the 5-minute product brief 3 times
- **Block B**: Record the final version
- **Block C**: Self-critique: where do you sound uncertain?
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 7 — Respond to hostile questions about your strategy
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 3 — Remove tentative language from a key paragraph
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 27 (Saturday)
- **Focus**: Simulated executive review. The final exam.
- **Block A**: Prepare for 15-minute executive review simulation
- **Block B**: Run the simulation (recorded or with peer)
- **Block C**: Self-assessment: what broke, what held
- **HIIT 1**: Drill 6 — Emergency compression under time pressure
- **HIIT 2**: Drill 5 — Final triage: what's in, what's out for the portfolio
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 28 (Sunday)
- **Focus**: Final weekly audit and public artifact
- **Morning**: Complete weekly audit
- **Afternoon**: **PUBLIC ARTIFACT #4** — Publish the product strategy memo or market thesis
- **Evening**: Plan the 90-day follow-on.
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB, PA

## Days 29-30: Integration and Exit

### Day 29 (Monday)
- **Focus**: Synthesis. What actually changed?
- **Block A**: Write the "what changed" memo
- **Block B**: Record a final 5-minute brief on your transformation
- **Block C**: Package the best artifacts into a portfolio
- **HIIT 1**: Final Drill 6 — Compress 30 days into 3 bullets
- **HIIT 2**: Final Drill 7 — Answer "why should I hire you?" with no hedging
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB

### Day 30 (Tuesday)
- **Focus**: Commitment. What stays, what goes, what's next.
- **Block A**: Write the 90-day follow-on plan
- **Block B**: Final scoreboard calculation
- **Block C**: Declare results to accountability partner
- **HIIT 1**: Final score review
- **HIIT 2**: Write one sentence: "I am now a ___ PM because ___"
- **Daily quotas**: D1, WA, VR, BC, AR, SB
