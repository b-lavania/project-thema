# Drill 3: Anti-Defensiveness Rewrite

## Setup
- Find something you wrote that feels important but soft
- Could be a resume bullet, positioning statement, case study, or recommendation

## The Form

**Original text:**

_________________________________________________

**Date:** ___________

---

### Step 1: Highlight the hedges (2 min)
Circle or list every:
- "I think" / "I believe"
- "might" / "could" / "possibly"
- "not just" / "actually" / "really"
- "somewhat" / "relatively"
- Any phrase that apologizes for the claim before making it
- Any "not X, but Y" structure that defends against an imaginary critic

Hedges found:
1.

3.

---

### Step 2: The brutal rewrite (3 min)
Same claim. No hedges. No throat-clearing. State it like it's already true.

_________________________________________________

---

### Step 3: Evidence check (2 min)
If the claim needs defending, is the problem the wording or the fact?

- [ ] Fact is solid. Rewrite was correct.
- [ ] Fact is weak. Fix the fact, not the wording.

---

## Self-check

- [ ] Original had hedges
- [ ] Rewrite has zero hedges
- [ ] Meaning preserved
- [ ] Sounds stronger, not just louder
