Example: Marketplace / operational analytics case study

We were seeing increasing operational friction in a last-mile delivery marketplace. At first glance, leadership thought it was mainly a staffing issue, but the data suggested the problem was actually upstream in the customer intake and dispatch process.

I started mapping the operational workflow end-to-end:

quote creation,
scheduling,
dispatch assignment,
driver preparation,
and escalation handling.

One issue was that the instrumentation was fragmented. Product events lived separately from CRM data, dispatch logs, and customer support interactions.

I consolidated multiple sources into a unified operational funnel so we could trace how uncertainty propagated through the system.

The analysis showed that jobs with incomplete inventory capture and ambiguous scheduling windows generated disproportionately high escalation rates and lower conversion.

We then redesigned parts of the intake flow and introduced AI-assisted workflows to improve inventory recognition and customer clarification earlier in the process.

Operational escalations dropped, booking flow efficiency improved, and dispatch teams spent less time manually correcting jobs.

Notice what this does:

business framing,
systems thinking,
operational reasoning,
instrumentation awareness,
analytics,
AI,
measurable outcomes.

That sounds much more senior than:

“I used analytics to improve the funnel.”
