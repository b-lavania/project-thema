# Cover Letter Style Guide (Google Docs–friendly)
Goal: Produce a one-page, ATS-safe cover letter that can be pasted directly into Google Docs (no Markdown styling required). Tone is confident, concise, and outcome/metrics-led for a competitive SF/tech audience.

## Length & format
- 230–320 words, 4–6 short paragraphs.
- Plain text only; avoid tables, special symbols, and heavy bullets. If bullets are needed, use simple hyphens.
- Single line breaks between paragraphs.

## Structure
1) Header block: Candidate name, email, phone, LinkedIn. Hiring manager name (if known), role title, company, date.
2) Hook (2–3 sentences): Mirror the company mission/problem; drop one strongest proof (metric) relevant to that mission.
3) Alignment (2–3 mini-paragraphs): Map 2–3 top duties to your evidence. Each mini-paragraph = duty → action you’ve done → conservative outcome with real numbers where possible.
4) Why this company/team (1 paragraph): Reference the mission/market, not generic culture; tie to a related portfolio proof.
5) Close (1 paragraph): Offer a next step (chat/intro call), mention availability/location constraints, thank them.

## Voice & rules
- First person, direct, concrete. Avoid buzzwords, fluff, and “passion” language unless the JD uses it explicitly.
- Lead with outcomes: speed, reliability, cost, revenue, deflection, cycle time, accuracy. Use conservative metrics from RES/extracted and portfolio files; do not invent numbers.
- Name the systems and levers: pricing architecture, operations research, computer vision, conversational AI, GTM automation, marketplace design, analytics/experimentation.
- Keep sentences tight (<2 lines). Prefer active verbs. Avoid adjective stacks.
- Keywording: weave JD terminology naturally; no keyword stuffing.
- If a detail is missing, flag it inline as “[Need detail: <item>]” rather than fabricating.

## Sources to pull from
- RES/extracted/*.txt for roles/companies, dates, baseline bullets, and skills.
- Portfolio/concept1–3.html for quantified proofs (e.g., 20× faster quoting, 85% on-time, ~50% support deflection, 3× take rate, $500k+ GMV, 14% retention lift, 7% conversion lift, 93% fill rate, energy modeling platform live link).
- Match the duty: pick the closest proof; keep outcomes conservative.

## Track-specific nudges
- Product/AI & Growth: emphasize shipping to production, experimentation loops, measurement, and user impact.
- Pricing/Ops & Logistics/Marketplace: emphasize quoting/pricing clarity, operational reliability, deflection/throughput, unit economics.
- BizOps / Chief of Staff: emphasize cross-functional execution, decision systems, narrative clarity, and measurable business lift.

## Output checklist (pre-flight)
- Word count in range; paragraphs separated by single blank lines.
- One strong hook metric; 2–3 aligned evidence mini-paragraphs; tailored “why this company” line.
- Plain text, ATS-safe characters; contact block present; no invented data.
