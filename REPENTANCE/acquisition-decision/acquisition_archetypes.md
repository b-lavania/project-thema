# Acquisition Archetype Screen

Screen **archetypes before specific deals.** Score each 1–5 on: unfair advantage, AI/product leverage post-close, product-led vs GM-led day-to-day, pigeonhole trap risk.

Only archetypes scoring **≥ 4 average** on fit + enjoyment survive to listing search.

---

## Scoring legend

| 1–5 | Unfair advantage | AI/product leverage | Day-to-day | Pigeonhole trap |
|-----|------------------|---------------------|------------|-----------------|
| 5 | You’ve done this exact problem | Core to value creation | Product/pricing led | Bounded, chosen domain |
| 3 | Adjacent skills | Nice-to-have | Mix | Unclear exit |
| 1 | Generic buyer | Irrelevant | Trucks, phones, HR | Indefinite grind |

---

## Archetype 1: Vertical SaaS with operational mess

**Examples:** Small logistics, field-service, dispatch, quoting, or back-office SaaS; $500K–$3M ARR; founder retiring; churn or pricing broken.

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Unfair advantage | **5** | Moovez/Quotely + BVXpress 0-to-1; pricing tier, workflow, onboarding |
| AI leverage | **5** | Your lane — agents, CV/OR, integration |
| Day-to-day | **4** | Product + customer + small team; not pure GM |
| Pigeonhole trap | **4** | Ops-AI/logistics adjacent if vertical chosen well |

**Risks:** Technical debt; need eng post-close; valuation multiples for micro-SaaS.

**Verdict:** **Best fit archetype.** Narrow search to verticals you’ve already shipped in.

## Archetype 2: Services business with pricing + workflow inefficiency

**Examples:** Moving company, freight broker ops, field-service contractor with manual quoting/dispatch; $1–5M revenue; owner-operator retiring.

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Unfair advantage | **4** | Quote-to-dispatch pattern from Moovez; OR/pricing |
| AI leverage | **4** | High if you productize intake/dispatch; low if pure labor shop |
| Day-to-day | **2** | Crews, trucks, calls, local ops — GM-heavy |
| Pigeonhole trap | **3** | Can become another indefinite ops cage |

**Risks:** You improve the business but **are** the dispatcher; conflicts with Founding Product Lead identity.

**Verdict:** **Conditional** — only if you buy to **install Quotely-style systems** and hire ops manager within 12 months; not if you are the ops.

## Archetype 3: Marketplace / logistics-adjacent operator

**Examples:** Regional brokerage, last-mile coordinator, 3PL lite; two-sided matching with manual back office.

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Unfair advantage | **5** | Direct Moovez experience; fill rate, quoting, TMS |
| AI leverage | **5** | Core wedge |
| Day-to-day | **3** | Ops + product; supply/demand balance |
| Pigeonhole trap | **4** | Same domain as locked 12-month bet — **conflicts with Path A** unless acquisition *is* the vehicle for Quotely |

**Risks:** Competes with Moovez/Quotely narrative; capital intensive; may duplicate current situation (weak eng).

**Verdict:** **Strategic only** — makes sense if structured as **acqui-hire of your own GTM** (buy small mover + deploy Quotely) with clear cap table story. Otherwise redundant with Path A.

## Archetype 4: M&A / diligence / back-office workflow business

**Examples:** Tools or services for deal advisors, PMI, workforce diligence; BVXpress-adjacent.

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Unfair advantage | **4** | 9 years BVXpress; HRIS for acquired cos |
| AI leverage | **3** | Document/workflow automation possible |
| Day-to-day | **3** | Product possible; many are services-heavy |
| Pigeonhole trap | **2** | Returns to finance/M&A lane you **left deliberately** |

**Risks:** `too-greedy.md` — distribution in finance feels like the cage you escaped.

**Verdict:** **Reject for 12-month goal** unless exit criteria written upfront and domain is **chosen**, not defaulted.

**Verdict:** **Reject** — fails `business_you_could_improve` gate unless price is distressed enough for financial engineering only (not your profile).

## Search filters (if proceeding to listings)

**Include:**
- SDE $150K–$400K (adjust after financing worksheet)
- Sectors: logistics tech, field service software, dispatch, quoting, freight ops software
- Seller financing or SBA-eligible
- Seller transition 3–6 months
- Clear workflow bottleneck you’ve solved before

**Exclude:**
- Multi-location retail without systems
- Heavy W2 workforce you must personally manage
- Unrelated domains (healthcare, restaurants, generic marketing agencies)
- Deals requiring $500K+ cash without committed financing
