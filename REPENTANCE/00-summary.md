**Phase 1: Build the list**

You're filtering for companies that match all of these simultaneously: Series-A (occasionally well-funded seed or early Series-B), AI-native in their core product, operational domain with real workflow complexity, 5+ engineers already in place, founded 2020-2024, North America with remote tolerance.

The domains to search within: last-mile logistics, freight/trucking operations, field service management, dispatch automation, supply chain visibility, fleet operations, warehouse workflow, B2B operations tooling.

Where to find them: Crunchbase filtered by sector + funding stage + raise date. YC company directory (batch 2021-2024, search logistics/ops/AI). Portfolio pages of firms that invest in this space — a16z, Bessemer, Initialized, Lerer Hippeau, and any logistics-focused fund like 8VC or Trucks VC. LinkedIn company search for headcount 10-50 in relevant sectors. TechCrunch funding announcements from the last 18 months.

You're looking for companies solving a problem you've already solved a version of. That's the filter, not just sector.

**Phase 2: Outreach**

Don't wait for job postings. Most Series-A companies in this space aren't posting PM roles formally, or if they are, the window is short and competitive. Go direct.

At Series-A: message the founder. At Series-B: message the Head of Product or VP Engineering. LinkedIn cold outreach works if the message is specific — "I built a CV + OR quoting system for last-mile logistics at Quotely, reduced job costing from 60 minutes to 3. I see you're working on [specific problem]. Worth a conversation?" That's it. No resume attachment in the first message.

Run batches of 15 at a time. Personalize each one. Track response rates and adjust the message.

**Phase 3: Resume**

Your resume needs one job: get you past the 10-second screen at a Series-A ops-AI company. That means a single clear headline, Quotely/Moovez leading with system-level outcomes, BVXpress framed as product leadership not Chief of Staff, and the fractional work compressed into supporting evidence rather than primary narrative.

It does not need to cover every project. It needs to make one argument: this person ships AI operational systems and measures the outcomes.
