Target Market Profile

Purpose:
This worksheet helps you clearly define who will value the problems you solve (Important Pain Points / IP), where they exist, and how to market yourself directly to them. Be specific — try to avoid guessing or generalities.


Step 1
Identify the Pain You Solve
1. Important Pain Points (IP):
What key challenges or problems do you want to be paid to solve?

Answer:
Operational ambiguity in quote-to-dispatch workflows — where manual intake, pricing, scheduling, crew assignment, and customer status create margin leakage, slow decisions, and support load. Specifically:
- Quote-to-dispatch latency (brokers/dispatchers spending 30–60+ min per job on costing, intake, or load building when it should take minutes)
- AI/voice/automation that fails at the handoff because structured job context never reaches the ops system (TMS, CRM, dispatch board)
- Pricing and margin decisions made on gut feel instead of lane-level or job-level confidence
- Workflow fragmentation across inbox, phone, spreadsheets, and legacy TMS — exception handling breaks, not feature gaps
- Product analytics blind spots: teams ship AI features without instrumentation to know what moves conversion, fill rate, or retention

Prompt: Think of the recurring problems you fix, the gaps others notice, or frustrations people bring to you.

Primary lane per REPENTANCE target list: AI-native logistics, dispatch, and field-service ops companies (Series-A to early Series-B).

Are there specific sectors, business units, or functions within these industries where these challenges are most common?

Answer:

- Product / engineering at companies where AI is core to the workflow product (not bolt-on chatbots)
- Back-office workflow automation (order entry, document extraction, billing reconciliation)

4. Company Size:

Answer:

- Revenue / funding: Series-A raise in last 18–36 months; actively scaling GTM and product, not maintenance mode
