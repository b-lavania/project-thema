# Grizzly Hunt — Branding Brief & Den Prep

Prep doc for Higher Landing / Grizzly Den. Answers are grounded in the [REPENTANCE](README.md) positioning work — sharp, honest, and built for a room that will push back.

**North star sentence:** I find the real operational bottleneck, reduce ambiguity, and build the system that makes the fix permanent.

**Primary identity:** Founding Product Lead for operational AI — logistics, freight, dispatch, field service.

---

## I. Brand Platform (Identity)

### Who are you?

Bharat "Bob" Lavania. Founding product operator with real technical depth — not a slide-deck PM. I've been the first product hire three times, shipped production AI systems (CV, OR, conversational agents, eval harnesses), and spent nine years building product at BVXpress before going fractional and landing on Moovez/Quotely.

I'm not a founder with a funding round. I'm the operator who built the product inside someone else's logistics business and proved it works in production.

### What do you do that's actually great? What's the big message?

The big message: **most teams are solving the wrong problem.** I find the structural bottleneck behind the stated one, then build the system that makes the fix stick.

Proof, not adjectives:

- Turned 60-minute phone quoting into 3-minute CV + OR job costing at Quotely
- Cut support load ~50% with structured conversational intake (not chatbot theater)
- Drove ~93% marketplace fill rate by reframing "compete on price" as "win on speed-to-quote"
- At Netsweeper, froze features and pruned false positives — 85% alert reduction, contract saved
- At Village Wellth, killed tier confusion — 3x take rate from decision architecture, not price tweaks

I speak technical and commercial. I can prototype the first version myself. That's rare in product.

### What do you really want? Primary career goal?

**A strong salaried role** as Founding PM, Principal Product, or Technical Product Owner at a company with a real engineering team — Series-A to early Series-B, AI-native, operational workflow complexity.

I need income in 1–3 months. I'm not here to "explore options." I'm here to land somewhere I can ship again without an absentee CTO blocking execution.

Secondary (bridge only): narrow consulting on agentic AI pilots — cash runway, not identity.

### What do you need to be happy?

- **Execution partners:** 5+ engineers who can build what I decide — the thing I don't have at Quotely
- **Conviction in the problem:** logistics, freight, dispatch, field ops — domains where my work maps directly
- **Autonomy with accountability:** own a number, not a backlog of clever ideas
- **Honest scale:** early enough that shipping track record matters, late enough that payroll is reliable

What I don't need: another fractional portfolio, another identity pivot, or "anything's better than current."

## II. Brand Snapshot

### Your story — how did you get here?

Finance-adjacent product builder → first product hire at BVXpress (employee #3, nine years, five products) → MBA and intentional exit from being pigeonholed in M&A SaaS → Netsweeper (AI compliance) + fractional across logistics, health, growth → Moovez/Quotely operator on equity, where I built the full AI layer in production.

The through-line isn't "lots of jobs." It's the same move every time: reject the brief, find the real problem, build the system, measure whether it worked.

### Where have you added exceptional value? (Career stories)

**Quotely / Moovez (present — lead with this)**  
Stated problem: compete on price. Real problem: speed-to-quote and job-cost trust. Built CV pipeline + OR engine + SMS agent + multi-model eval framework. Job costing 60 min → 3 min. ~50% support deflection. ~93% fill rate. Blocked now by absentee CTO — resource constraint, not capability failure.

**BVXpress (2012–2021)**  
Employee #3. Built the product function from scratch. Launched five products, 1,400+ users. HRIS for M&A transaction readiness — adapter architecture, not rip-and-replace. Owned distribution in finance/M&A for nine years.

**Netsweeper (2022–2023)**  
Stated problem: detection accuracy. Real problem: operational trust. Froze feature creep, pruned false-alert vectors. 85% reduction, district trust restored, contract renewed.

**Village Wellth (fractional)**  
Stated problem: pricing. Real problem: decision architecture. Consolidated tiers, 3x take rate.

**Telehealth (TechStars Calgary 2024)**  
Won pitch competition. Government cancelled program before launch — strong design evidence, weak outcome evidence. Mention briefly, don't lead with it.

### Why are you here?

Because cold, undifferentiated job search doesn't work for my profile, and I need help sharpening the story for rooms that will ask hard questions.

I have the technical receipts and the reframe skill. I don't have Fortune 500 logos or a breakout exit. Grizzly Den is where I pressure-test the narrative before founders and hiring managers do it for me.

## III. Brand Personality (Value Proposition)

### What makes you stand out from peers?

**Diagnostic reframing + ships in production.** Most PMs manage roadmaps. I dissolve the wrong problem and build the first system myself.

Three words: **reframe, build, commercialize.**

### What is your value proposition?

> I'm a founding product lead. I join messy early-stage products, find the real problem behind the stated one — using analytics and research, not instinct — and build the system that makes the fix stick. I've done it as employee #3, #6, and as the only product hire.

### Why should I hire you?

Because you've already tried hiring a generalist PM to "figure out AI," and they're still writing PRDs while your dispatchers quote by phone.

I've already shipped CV + OR quoting, conversational intake, and eval harnesses in a live logistics operation. I know where agentic AI breaks in the field — unstructured handoffs, exception handling, trust in the signal — not just in a demo.

### What am I guaranteed to get from you?

- Honest problem diagnosis in the first 30 days — including "stop building that"
- A production-minded path to automation, not a strategy deck
- Metrics tied to operational outcomes (time-to-quote, deflection, fill rate, trust) — not vanity AI metrics
- Someone who will prototype to settle arguments instead of scheduling more alignment meetings

What you're not guaranteed: org-building at Director scale, FAANG polish, or pretending $500K GMV is a unicorn story.

## IV. Employer Persona (Ideal Employer)

### Who is your ideal employer?

**Series-A to early Series-B**, North America, remote-tolerant, **5+ engineers in place**, AI-native core product, operational workflow complexity.

**Domains:** last-mile logistics, freight/trucking, dispatch automation, field service, supply chain visibility, B2B ops tooling.

**Titles:** Founding PM, Principal Product, Head of AI Product, Technical Product Owner (agentic systems).

**Also in play:** mid-large companies with serious agentic AI initiatives (warm lead lane) — but that's one opportunity, not the strategy.

**Not targeting:** FAANG, late-stage growth PM, Director/Head of Product (no managing-managers track record), undifferentiated job boards.

### How would you add value to them?

I shorten the loop between "we should automate this" and "operators actually use it."

Concrete pattern:

1. Map where judgment-dependent manual work creates latency (quoting, dispatch, intake, exception routing)
2. Reframe to the real bottleneck (usually trust or decision architecture, not missing features)
3. Ship the smallest production system that proves the fix
4. Measure adoption and unit economics — kill what doesn't move a number

### Who do you want to work for — and why?

**Founders and technical leaders** at ops-AI companies who've raised enough to hire but still feel the pain of phone-tag quoting, inbox chaos, and dispatcher distrust of "AI recommendations."

**Why them:** my Quotely work is a compressed preview of their roadmap. I add domain specificity most PM candidates lack — production AI in logistics, not slideware.

**Why not others:** large-company hiring funnels screen on logos I don't have. Seed-stage with no eng team repeats my current CTO problem.

**Named direction (batch 1):** Greenscreens, Vooma, HappyRobot, FleetWorks, Avoca AI, BuildOps, Supermove, Alvys — see [`company-targets.md`](company-targets.md).

## V. Brand Commitment (Convince Me)

### What do you need to be convinced of?

That the company has **engineering execution**, not just AI ambition. I've lived the alternative — built the product, blocked on shipping.

Also: that the role owns a real outcome, not a feature factory. I need a number, a team that can build, and a founder who wants the fix, not the theater.

### What's your ideal vision of yourself?

**12 months from now:** one identity a stranger can repeat back — "founding product lead who ships operational AI in logistics." One employer or one ARR number large enough I don't recycle the same five metrics. Deep enough in one domain that I have opinions operators respect, not just frameworks.

### Why do you want it?

Because insight without compounding is a hobby, and I'm tired of clever small wins. I want to hold one bet through the boring middle and let it get big.

### What makes you think you can achieve it?

I've done the 0-to-1 part repeatedly. The gap isn't skill — it's commitment and distribution. REPENTANCE names that honestly. This plan is the corrective: one domain, one identity, one number, 12 months.

The technical proof exists. The narrative is now sharp enough to sell. What's left is reps — outbound, interviews, closing.

### In your ideal situation, what can you be counted on for?

- Finding the real problem before the team burns a quarter on the wrong one
- Shipping production systems, not perpetual pilots
- Tying work to operational metrics leadership actually tracks
- Staying when it gets boring — that's the commitment I'm making here
