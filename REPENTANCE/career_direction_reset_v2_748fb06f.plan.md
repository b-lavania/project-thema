# Career Direction Reset (v2)

## Situation (corrected)

You are an early-stage operator at Quotely/Moovez on equity only, no cash. You built the AI product systems (CV pipeline, OR engine, SMS agent, eval harnesses). The CTO is absentee but gets featured in fundraising decks. The team may not be capable of understanding or selling what you built. Enterprise sales cycles are long and you have no runway to wait them out.

You need reliable income within 1-3 months. Cold outreach is not working. You have one warm lead (Technical Product Owner, agentic AI, mid-large company) past the recruiter screen. Former founders you have worked with would hire you but lack funding.

## The Core Strategic Call

**Get a strong salaried role. That is the primary objective.**

Everything else -- consulting, Quotely, portfolio work -- is either preparation for that objective, or a bridge to it, or a hedge against it taking longer than expected. Do not confuse the bridge with the destination.

Quotely/Moovez stays on your record as proof of execution. It does not stay on your calendar as the main bet. You are blocked by an absentee CTO, on equity with no cash, at a company that may not be able to sell what you built. That is a clean, defensible exit. Use it.

## The Honest Topology: One Opportunity + Two Motions

### The warm lead (priority, but not a strategy)

You are right that one company past a recruiter screen is not a lane. It is a lottery ticket that happens to be your highest-probability single outcome right now. Treat it accordingly:

- Prepare thoroughly. Use the exit narrative from REPENTANCE: "I built Quotely's AI product systems from scratch, proved they work in production, and I'm blocked by an absentee CTO who can't execute on the engineering side."
- Lead with system metrics (60 min to 3 min, 50% deflection, 93% fill rate), not GMV.
- Position yourself as someone who has already shipped agentic AI systems in production and understands where they break, what users actually adopt, and how to scope pilots that survive contact with real teams.
- Prepare for the three interview risks from REPENTANCE: (1) your actual title/comp structure at Moovez, (2) why you did not fix the CTO bottleneck, (3) the small-n scale question.
- Do not slow down anything else while waiting on this process. One process is never a plan.

### Motion 1: Targeted job search (primary)

This replaces undifferentiated cold outreach with a narrow, network-activated search.

**Who you are targeting:**
- Series-A to early Series-B companies (5+ engineers) building AI-native operational tooling.
- Domains: logistics, supply chain, field service, dispatch automation, B2B workflow automation.
- Titles: Technical Product Owner, PM for AI/agentic systems, Founding PM, Head of AI Product.
- Also: mid-large companies with agentic AI initiatives where a Technical Product Owner or AI PM role exists (this is where the warm lead sits; there are more like it).

**How to activate warm intros (the specific mechanic):**

The founders who would hire you but lack funding are your intro network. They cannot hire you, but they know people who can. The ask is not "do you know anyone who's hiring?" -- that is too vague and gets nothing. The ask is:

> "I'm targeting Series-A ops-AI companies with real engineering teams, building workflow automation or logistics tooling. Who in your network is either (a) hiring for product, or (b) a founder/VP Eng at a company that matches? I only need 2-3 names."

Run this with every founder, operator, and manager you have worked with. Each conversation should produce 2-3 intros. 5 conversations = 10-15 intros. That is a pipeline.

**Parallel channels (not cold outreach):**
- REPENTANCE's 50-80 company list approach: Crunchbase, YC directory (batch 2021-2024), VC portfolio pages (a16z, Bessemer, Initialized, 8VC, Trucks VC), LinkedIn headcount 10-50.
- Direct-to-founder messages when you have domain relevance: "I built a CV + OR quoting system for last-mile logistics at Quotely, reducing job costing from 60 min to 3. I see you're working on [specific problem]. Worth a conversation?"
- This is not cold outreach in the generic sense. It is domain-specific, metric-led, and targeted. The difference matters.

**What you are NOT doing:**
- Broad job-board applications to companies where your profile does not fit their screening filters.
- Generic PM applications to large companies with structured hiring funnels (you do not have the logos to pass screening there).
- Applications for Director/Head of Product roles (the org-building gap is real; do not expose it).

### Motion 2: Consulting bridge (secondary, time-boxed)

This is a cash bridge, not an identity. It buys time if the job search takes longer than 90 days. It is NOT a career direction.

**One narrow offer:**
- "AI workflow discovery + prototype plan in 2-3 weeks" or "Product owner for agentic pilot, scope to first internal launch."
- Sell outcomes, not capability range. One deliverable, one price, one timeline.

**Who to sell to:**
- Former colleagues, operators, and founders who already trust your judgment.
- Specifically: the same founders who would hire you but lack funding may have consulting budget, or know someone who does.
- Companies exploring agentic AI internally who need someone to de-risk the first pilot.

**Time-box:** Do not spend more than 20% of your weekly hours on consulting pipeline. If a paid engagement lands, take it, but do not let it consume the job search.

## Rules for Quotely / Moovez

- You are an operator on equity, not a founder. Frame it that way everywhere.
- Default posture: maintain the product, extract proof points, do not invest new build hours.
- Do not pursue enterprise sales conversations unless a buyer shows real urgency, access, and budget unprompted.
- The clean exit narrative: "I built the AI product systems, proved they work, and I'm blocked by an absent CTO. That's a resource constraint, not a capability failure."
- If Quotely finds real traction during this period, reassess. But do not bet your next 90 days on it.

## Weekly Cadence

Every Monday, answer three questions:
1. How many targeted conversations (intros, applications, follow-ups) did I have this week? Target: 5+.
2. Is the warm lead still progressing? What is the next step and when?
3. Am I spending time on things that do not move toward income? Cut them.

## What Success Looks Like

- **Near term (30 days):** 10+ targeted conversations in pipeline. Warm lead progressing or dead (either outcome is useful). Consulting offer defined and tested with 3 trusted contacts.
- **60 days:** At least one job process in mid-to-late stage, OR one paid consulting engagement landed.
- **90 days:** Offer in hand or imminent. If not, reassess whether the target profile needs widening.
