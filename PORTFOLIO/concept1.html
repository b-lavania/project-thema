The Problem

Moovez, a last-mile delivery brokerage, was quoting jobs manually. Every estimate required a dispatcher to spend an average of one hour on phone calls, visual guesswork, and spreadsheet arithmetic.

The quotes were unreliable, leading to billing disputes and unpredictable scheduling. The support burden grew linearly with volume, creating a bottleneck that prevented the company from scaling efficiently.

The Approach

Instead of patching the existing process, I rebuilt the estimation and operations workflow from first principles. By leveraging historical job data, computer vision, and operations research, I defined three interconnected systems.

The goal was to transform a manual, subjective process into an algorithm-grounded, automated engine that could be commercialized as a standalone SaaS: Quotely.

The Solution

Three Interconnected Systems

Computer Vision
Visual Cost Estimation Tool

Replaced manual inventory calls with a CV model. Customers photograph items; the model estimates volume and complexity to generate instant quotes.

Operations Research
Job-Time & Mover Allocation

A predictive framework solving duration, optimal mover count, and 3D bin packing—trained on years of historical job performance data.

Conversational AI
SMS-Based Support Agent

Built an automated SMS agent handling bookings, schedule changes, and queries, integrated directly with the operations backend.

"I engineered this entire system as a single contributor—from mining data to defining the predictive frameworks and driving production deployment."
