The Breakdown

Manual job quoting is a hidden tax on operations. Every estimate that takes an hour (built from phone calls, visual guesswork, and spreadsheet arithmetic) is an hour of decision latency.

When requirements don't exist and strategy isn't defined, many become uncomfortable. I redefined this problem to focus on the entire revenue system, not just the quote form.

The Synthesis

The solution wasn't execution; it was understanding. I connected business strategy to technical execution by designing three interconnected systems that scale without proportional headcount.

Resulting system now commercialized as Quotely, achieving $500k+ GMV through architectural thinking.

System Nodes

Logistics / CV-Node
Visual Cost Estimation

Replaced manual guesswork with a CV model. Customers photograph items; the system estimates volume instantly.

Logistics / OR-Engine
Predictive Allocation

Solving duration, crew count, and 3D bin packing using years of historical performance data.

Logistics / Agent-Node
SMS Support Agent

Automated bookings and schedule changes via an SMS AI agent integrated with the operations backend.

Pattern recognition: This same problem class appears in field service scheduling, home service quoting, healthcare appointment optimization, and on-demand delivery.

Same solution architecture: Computer vision for estimation + operations research for optimization + conversational AI for customer interaction.

Same economic outcome: 10-20× efficiency improvement, 50%+ cost reduction, predictable scheduling.

Engineered end-to-end by a single contributor, from mining historical job data and defining the predictive framework, through rapid prototyping, to driving engineering collaboration for production deployment. The resulting system is now commercialized as Quotely, sold as a standalone SaaS to other operators.
